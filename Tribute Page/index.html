MUHAMMAD ALI

Muhammad Ali was an American professional boxer and activist.
Nicknamed "the Greatest", he is regarded as one of the most significant sports figures of the 20th century.
He is often regarded as the greatest heavyweight boxer of all time.

Here is life journey of Muhammad Ali

Birth and Early Years (1942-1960)

  • Born on January 17, 1942, Louisville, Kentuckey.
  • Started boxing at the age of 12.
  • Won a gold medal in the light heavyweight division at the 1960 Summer Olympics in Rome

Conversion to Islam and Name Change (1961)

  • Converted to Islam and joined the Nation of Islam.
  • Changed his name to Muhammad Ali.

Heavyweight Champion (1964)

  • Defeated Sonny Liston in 1964 to become the youngest ever heavyweight champion at the age of 22.

Refusal to Serve in the Vietnam War (1967)

  • Refused to be drafted into the military during the Vietnam War, citing religious reasons and opposition to the war.
  • Stripped of his boxing titles and banned from the sport for several years

Legal Battles and Comeback (1970-1974)

  • Fought legal battles to regain his boxing license and right to compete
  • Retured to the ring in 1970 and reclaimed the heavyweight title in 1974 by defeating George Foreman in the famous "Rumble in the Jungle".

Later Boxing Career and Retirement (1975-1981)

  • Fought in notable matches, including the "Thrilla in Manila" against Joe Frazier.
  • Retired from professional boxing in 1981 with a record of 56 wins and 5 losses.

Impact and Legacy

  • Known for his charisma, trash-talking, and poetic expression.
  • Considered one of the greatest boxers of all time
  • Became a global ambassador for peace, civil rights, and humanitarian causes.

Health Struggles and Death (1984-2016)

  • Diagnosed with Parkinson's disease, likely due to the head trauma suffered during his boxing career.
  • Devoted time to philanthropy and humanitarian work.
  • Passed away on June 3, 2016, in Scottsdale, Arizona.
